There is a moment, somewhere in the middle of building something, when you forget that you are building it. The work stops being a plan and starts being a thing. You stop making decisions about it and start listening to it. This is the moment I am always chasing.

I have been making things for as long as I can remember. Drawings, songs, software, furniture, websites, arguments, meals. The medium changes but the feeling does not. There is something underneath all of it that stays constant, some shared grammar of creation that I have been slowly trying to learn.

## The first version is wrong

Every first version is wrong. This is not a deficiency of skill or effort. It is a structural feature of making things. You cannot know what something wants to be until it partially exists. The first version is how you find out.

The mistake people make is treating the first version as a failure. It is not a failure. It is research. It is the thing telling you what it needs. The experienced maker has learned to treat the first version as a conversation opener, not a verdict.

This is why perfectionism at the start is so dangerous. If you try to make the first version perfect, you are trying to skip the research phase. You are refusing to listen. You end up with something technically correct and spiritually empty — a thing that was made according to a plan rather than in response to itself.

> The first cut is not the edit. The first draft is not the argument. The first build is not the product. These are all attempts at starting a conversation.

The question is always: can you start that conversation cheaply enough to have many of them?

## Constraints are not enemies

The best work I have made has always happened under constraints. Deadline, budget, technology limitation, physical material — it does not matter what form the constraint takes. What matters is that something is saying no to your easiest options.

When everything is possible, nothing is necessary. The constraint collapses the solution space down to something navigable. It forces specificity. A good constraint is not a cage. It is a question.

The constraints I resist are always the ones I eventually understand. I have spent years fighting the limitations of the web as a medium, wishing it were more like print, wishing I had more control over typography, wishing pixels behaved like ink. And in those years of resistance I missed the fact that the web was trying to tell me something — that its real power is in its relationality, its linkedness, its ability to be read in a thousand different contexts by a thousand different people on a thousand different devices.

The medium was offering me something I did not want. Once I stopped refusing it, I could use it.

## The part that comes from somewhere else

There is a phase in making that I cannot explain and have stopped trying to. It is the phase when something unexpected arrives — a connection you did not plan, a solution that emerges from the problem rather than from your deliberate thinking, a line that is better than anything you could have written on purpose.

I do not know where this comes from. I am suspicious of mystical explanations, but I am equally suspicious of purely mechanistic ones. What I know is that it tends to arrive when you are working rather than thinking about working. It does not come when you are planning. It comes when your hands are busy.

This is the best argument against overthinking I know. The part of making that feels most like discovery — the part that makes you feel briefly like you have access to something larger than yourself — that part requires that you be in motion. It will not come to you sitting still.

---

I do not know what I am making most of the time. I have a vague sense of direction and a collection of tools and a feeling that something wants to exist. That is usually enough to start. The rest is listening.

The things I have made that I am most proud of share one quality: somewhere in their making, they surprised me. They became something I did not know I was building. That surprise is what I am looking for. Everything else is just the path to it.
